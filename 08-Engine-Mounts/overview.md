## Why I'm Doing This

At 170k miles, my stock rubber mounts are almost certainly degraded and soft. Stiffer mounts will reduce engine movement under load, which means:
- Better shift feel (less engine rock during gear changes)
- Improved throttle response (less drivetrain slop)
- Less wheel hop under hard launches
- Ties everything together with the Acuity short shifter and bushings for an overall tighter drivetrain feel

This is the last piece of the drivetrain feel puzzle. Once these go in alongside the clutch, flywheel, short shifter, and bushings that are already on the car, it should feel like a completely different machine.

## Trade-Offs

70A is on the **streetable end** of performance mounts:

| Durometer | Character |
|-----------|-----------|
| Stock rubber (~40-50A) | Smooth, quiet, isolates all vibration |
| **70A (this kit)** | **Noticeable vibration at idle, moderate NVH increase, significant improvement in drivetrain feel** |
| 85-90A | Harsh at idle, very noticeable cabin vibration, track-oriented |
| Solid (100A+) | Race car. Unbearable for street use. |

**What I'm expecting:** A noticeable buzz/vibration at idle and low RPM, especially through the steering wheel, shift knob, and pedals. At driving RPMs it should disappear. Most 8th gen SI owners running 70A mounts say it's a worthwhile trade-off for the improved driving feel, and I'm fine with a little vibration at idle.

## Notes

- No tuning impact — purely mechanical
- Install requires supporting the engine from above (engine support bar) or below (jack under oil pan with wood block) while swapping mounts
- Typical install time: 2-4 hours
