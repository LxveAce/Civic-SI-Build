# Purple Calipers — Phased Plan

## Owner's Decision

- **NOW (Option A):** purple caliper paint on stock calipers when rear pads/rotors are installed. Keeps the 3-day-old fresh front brake job intact.
- **FUTURE (Option C):** plan a full Wilwood / StopTech / Brembo big-brake kit for a later phase (~260+ WHP or ~40-50k miles from now). **Do not buy now.** Documented here so the decision is already researched when the time comes.
- **NOT DOING (Option B):** full Wilwood front kit right now — rejected. Would throw away the Hawk HPS + R1 Concepts rotors installed 2026-04-20.

## Part 1 — NOW: G2 Purple Caliper Paint Kit

### What to buy

| Item | Part # | Source | Price |
|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **G2 High Temp Epoxy Paint — Purple** | **G2165** | Amazon / Walmart / G2USA direct | ~$55-75 |
| Prep supplies (brake cleaner 2x, red Scotchbrite, painter's tape) | — | Any auto parts | ~$25 |
| Nitrile gloves (hardener is nasty) | — | Hardware store | ~$12 |
| **One kit covers all 4 corners.** | | | |
| **Total** | | | **~$95-120** |

G2165 is the official G2 Purple kit: 3.5 oz epoxy base + 0.5 oz hardener = 4 oz mixed, 980°F rating (overkill for Hawk HPS street use), 2-hour wheel-on cure, 24-hour drive cure.

### Why paint instead of covers

| Factor | G2 Paint (winner) | MGP Covers (rejected) |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Cost | $95-120 | $400-500 (custom PPG purple + Si logo) |
| Lead time | Buy today | 12 business days custom color |
| Slotted rotor visible | YES (my R1 Concepts stays visible) | No — cover hides caliper body |
| Install timing | Do during rear brake install | Separate labor day |
| Long-term durability | Epoxy won't flake if prepped correctly | Clips can rattle or pop off at speed |
| Reversibility | None (acceptable — calipers are at 170k) | Fully reversible |

### Alternatives considered and rejected

- **Foliatec Deep Violet FT-2179** ($50-80, 572°F rating): great color, 3-part kit with cleaner + hardener. Slightly lower heat rating than G2 but still plenty for Hawk HPS street use. Brush application only. Buy this if G2 is out of stock.
- **Dupli-Color Plum Purple DE1640** ($10-15): NOT marketed as caliper-specific. High Temp Coating intended for engines. Used on calipers commonly but chips where pads contact bracket. Recoat every 2-3 years.
- **Plasti-Dip purple**: peels within 30 days on brake calipers. Don't.
- **VHT Caliper Paint**: no purple SKU in their SP line. Black/red/blue/gold/yellow/silver only.

### In-car paint procedure (rotors + pads stay on)

1. Work one corner at a time. Car stays anchored on the other three.
2. Park flat, chock opposite wheels.
3. Loosen lugs 1/2 turn while wheel is still on ground.
4. Jack up, support on jack stand. **Never trust the jack alone.**
5. Remove wheel.
6. **Mask everything not going purple:**
   - Rotor face (both sides) — slide a disc of scrap cardboard between pad and rotor, or mask the edge with tape
   - Brake pads and backing plates
   - Brake hose
   - Dust shield (optional — if you want it clean)
   - Slider boots (heat-sensitive rubber — do NOT paint)
7. **Clean caliper thoroughly** with brake cleaner. Spray → drip → spray again. Keep cleaner OFF the pad friction material.
8. **Wire-brush loose rust** on visible surfaces. Don't worry about behind the caliper — invisible through the wheel anyway.
9. **Scuff with red Scotchbrite.** Kill the gloss, give the paint a key.
10. **Final brake-cleaner wipe.** Spotless. No oil, no fingerprints.
11. **Mix G2 kit** per instructions (hardener into paint bottle, cap, shake 2 minutes).
12. **Apply in thin even coats.** 2-3 coats minimum. 15-20 min tack time between coats.
13. **Don't drip onto the rotor surface.** If you do, wipe immediately with brake cleaner on a rag.
14. **Wait 2 hours** before reinstalling wheel.
15. **Reinstall wheel**, torque lugs to **80 ft-lbs** (FG2 spec). Move to next corner.
16. **First drive:** keep it gentle for 50 miles — no hard stops. Let the epoxy finish fully cross-link under normal heat cycling.
17. **Don't power-wash** the calipers for 7 days.

### Ideal timing — paint when installing rear brakes

The rear pads (HB145F.570) + rotors (part of WCPN2-59004 set) are still in the box. Do them together:
1. Unbox rear pads + rotors
2. Jack up all 4 corners over a weekend, supported properly
3. Install rear rotors + pads
4. **While all 4 calipers are exposed**, paint them G2 purple
5. Let cure overnight
6. Wheels back on, bed in all four corners together with Hawk's factory bed-in procedure

Single weekend, one paint mix, zero wasted labor.

### Skip list

- **AP Racing Essex Radi-CAL** — no FG2 kit; custom bracket build only ($4,500-6,500). Track-only. Not for this build unless it becomes a dedicated track car.
- **TSX caliper OEM swap** — cheap ($500 all-in) mid-step upgrade but not a big-brake kit. Worth noting as an option if budget is the constraint.

### What gets thrown away at BBK time

- Hawk HPS pads front + rear (~$222 sunk; maybe $50-100 resale if low wear)
- R1 Concepts slotted rotors (~$310 sunk; maybe $50-100 resale if straight)
- Stock calipers (core value only, ~$20)
- Stock rubber brake lines (trash — 17 years old at 170k, they need replacing anyway)
- G2 purple paint on calipers (gone with the calipers)

**Effective sunk cost if BBK happens in Year 2-4:** ~$380-430. **This is exactly why the recommendation is G2 paint ($95) instead of MGP custom covers ($500) for Option A** — minimize throwaway.

### Phased approach recommendation

- **Years 0-2, FBO + tune, <230 WHP:** Hawk HPS + R1 Concepts + G2 purple. Total brake investment: ~$620.
- **Years 2-4, post-full-BO + E85:** reassess. If Hawk HPS shows fade on canyon runs (IR-thermometer the rotor after a spirited descent), upgrade to Hawk HP+ or HPS5.0 pads ($200) before committing to a BBK.
- **Year 4-5 or at 260+ WHP or first track day:** full Wilwood FNSL 6R + D154 rear, custom purple anodize (~$3,000-3,300 all-in).
- **Track-only future:** AP Racing Radi-CAL only if the car becomes a dedicated track build.
