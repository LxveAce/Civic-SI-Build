## About Belt Retention (Raised Lips)

I went down a rabbit hole on this, so here's what I found. Serpentine pulleys use **multi-rib grooves** (the V-shaped channels) as the belt retention system. The ribs on the belt interlock with the grooves on the pulley — that's what prevents slip and lateral walk. Raised flanges on the outer edges are only found on **smooth/flat idler pulleys** (which have no ribs), not on ribbed pulleys.

The stock automatic tensioner's idler pulley already has flanged edges because it's a smooth-face pulley. The ribbed pulleys (crank, alternator) don't need flanges because the groove profile does the job.

**Belt walk is caused by:**
- Misalignment between pulleys (not a concern with bolt-on replacements designed for the K20Z3)
- Worn/stretched belt (just replace the belt when installing new pulleys)
- Worn automatic tensioner (inspect at install — replace if it doesn't spring back firmly)

**Bottom line:** With properly machined pulleys (ATI + NST), a fresh serpentine belt, and a functioning auto-tensioner, belt walk isn't a concern. The grooves ARE the retention system.

## What Underdrive Pulleys Actually Do

The K20Z3 uses a single serpentine belt to drive all accessories. These accessories consume engine power (parasitic loss):

| Accessory | Parasitic Loss at WOT |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Alternator | 1-3 HP |
| A/C Compressor | 3-8 HP (when engaged) |
| Power Steering Pump | 1-3 HP |
| Water Pump | 1-2 HP |
| **Total** | **~6-16 HP** |

Underdrive pulleys spin accessories ~15% slower, recovering 2-5 HP. The lightweight aluminum construction also reduces rotational inertia, improving throttle response. Community consensus: **~80-85% of the felt gain comes from weight reduction** (faster revving), ~15-20% from actual underdrive.

## Why I'm NOT Deleting the Harmonic Damper

A lot of aftermarket crank pulleys (Buddy Club, NST, Unorthodox Racing) are solid billet aluminum with **no rubber isolator and no damping.** They delete the harmonic damper entirely.

**At 170k miles, I'm not willing to take that risk:**
- My crankshaft has already accumulated 170k miles of fatigue cycles
- I'm planning mods (headers, aggressive timing, higher RPM operation) that increase torsional vibrations
- Uncontrolled vibrations accelerate main bearing wear, timing chain stretch, and crankshaft fatigue
- The ATI provides better-than-stock damping while still being lighter — there's literally no reason to delete the damper when this option exists

The K-series community debates this endlessly. No confirmed crankshaft failures from billet pulleys exist. But the ATI costs ~$100-200 more than a billet pulley and eliminates the risk entirely. At 170k miles, that's cheap insurance.

## Compatibility Notes

### Alternator Charging with LattePanda
The NST overdrive alternator pulley spins the alternator **faster** than stock, maintaining full charging capacity even with the LattePanda drawing 15-30W continuously. I'll monitor battery voltage after install — should remain above 13.8V at idle with all accessories on.

### A/C Function
The slight underdrive means the A/C compressor spins ~15% slower. At idle in extreme heat (100F+), cooling may be marginally reduced. Above ~1500 RPM, no perceptible difference. I can live with that.

### Power Steering
Slightly heavier steering at parking-lot speeds. At any speed above ~5 MPH, unnoticeable. I actually prefer slightly heavier steering feel anyway.

### Drive-by-Wire
Zero effect — throttle is electronically actuated, not connected to the pulley system.

## Installation

### Difficulty: Easy-Moderate (2-3 hours DIY)

I'm planning to do this myself. Straightforward job.

### Tools Needed
- Impact wrench or long breaker bar + cheater pipe (crank bolt is ~180+ ft-lbs)
- Harmonic balancer puller (rent free from AutoZone)
- 19mm socket (crank bolt)
- Torque wrench
- Standard socket set for alternator and idler bolts

### Steps
1. Remove serpentine belt (release auto tensioner with wrench)
2. Remove crank bolt (19mm, impact wrench recommended)
3. Use harmonic balancer puller to remove stock crank pulley — **DO NOT pry** (damages crank seal)
4. Install ATI Super Damper, torque crank bolt to spec
5. Swap alternator pulley to NST unit
6. Swap idler pulley to NST unit
7. Install new serpentine belt (stock OEM or Gates K070663), verify routing
8. Start engine, verify belt tracking, listen for noise
9. Re-check belt tension after 100 miles
