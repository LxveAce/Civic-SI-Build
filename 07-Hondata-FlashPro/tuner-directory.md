## Tuner Selection Criteria — What Matters

### Non-Negotiable

1. **Returns my stock calibration backup on request.** If they won't save and hand back my OEM `.fpc`, walk away.
2. **Uses datalogs during the tune.** Not just dyno sheets — actual parameter-level logs. This is the work product.
3. **Runs multiple baseline pulls before any changes.** If their first move is "let me advance some timing," find another shop.
4. **Documents what they changed.** I want a calibration-diff I can review.
5. **Has public dyno results on 8thcivic/Honda-Tech/YouTube.** Verifiable track record, not Instagram marketing.

### Strongly Preferred

1. **Specializes in K-series / 8th gen Si specifically.** Shops that tune 50 platforms and barely see SIs will miss K20Z3-specific quirks.
2. **Writes detailed tune notes.** Not "dyno'd at 215 whp, see you next time."
3. **Honors the reliability-first rule.** Tuner agrees before the session that my 170k-mile motor gets safe AFR/timing, not max-possible AFR/timing.
4. **Hondata FlashPro native** — they tune Hondata every week, not once a year.

### Red Flags (Walk Away)

- Quotes a peak WHP guarantee before seeing the car
- Won't show me a recent datalog from another customer
- Pushes upsells (cams, turbo kit) before even doing the base tune
- Reviews on 8thcivic or r/CivicSi include the word "melted"
- No mention of knock count or AFR safety margins on their site
- Very cheap relative to others (~$200 when everyone else is $500)
- Won't tune my specific hardware combo ("we don't really work with Skunk2 alphas")

## What Each Session Should Cost

### Tune #1: Post-Exhaust (Headers + HFC + Cutout)

- Dual calibration (Daily quiet + Sport with cutout open)
- Pump gas only
- **Expected:** $400-600 in-shop, $300-450 remote
- **Session length:** 2-4 hours on dyno
- **What gets tuned:** fuel tables (low-cam + high-cam), ignition timing, VTEC engagement, rev hang, torque management flattening for Sport map

### Tune #2: Post-Intake Manifold (Full Bolt-On Pump Gas)

- Dual calibration (Daily + Sport)
- Still pump gas
- **Expected:** $400-600 in-shop, $300-450 remote
- **Session length:** 2-4 hours
- **What gets tuned:** everything from Tune #1 refined, plus IACV recalibration (new IM), IMRC defeat flag, potentially VTC phase adjustments for Ultra Street manifold

### Tune #3: Flex Fuel (Gas + E85 Dual-Fuel Interpolation)

- Dual calibration (Daily + Sport) × dual fuel (gas + E85 endpoints) = **4 effective tunes**
- **Expected:** $500-800 in-shop, $400-600 remote
- **Session length:** 4-6+ hours (flex fuel is the long day)
- **What gets tuned:** E85 endpoint fuel + timing tables, sensor calibration verification, cold-start enrichment, interpolation verification with gradual E-content changes

### Combined Pricing?

Some shops offer a package across all three sessions for a ~10-15% discount. **I'm not interested** — separation of hardware changes means clean attribution of gains. Combined sessions also make troubleshooting harder if something regresses between changes.

## Dyno Type — What to Expect

| Dyno | Character | Typical K20Z3 FBO Reading |
|------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Dynojet** (inertia roller) | Reads highest, most common for K-series bench-racing comparisons | 210-225 WHP / 150-160 WTQ |
| **Mustang** (eddy-current load) | Reads 5-12 WHP lower than Dynojet on same car | 200-215 WHP / 145-155 WTQ |
| **Dynapack** (hub dyno) | Best repeatability, slightly lower readings than Dynojet | 200-215 WHP / 145-155 WTQ |

**Honest expectation for a well-tuned FBO K20Z3 in 2026:** 210-225 WHP on a Dynojet. Anything above 240 WHP pump gas on stock internals is suspect or built.

E85 flex fuel adds 10-18 WHP typically. **Above 250 WHP pump, below 265 WHP E85 is the realistic envelope for NA FBO.**

## Questions to Ask Every Shop Before Booking

1. "Can you show me a recent datalog from a 06-11 Civic SI K20Z3 FBO tune?" (Verifies they actually tune these)
2. "Do you save and return the stock calibration to the customer?" (Non-negotiable)
3. "What's your approach when you see knock counts in a datalog?" (Answer should include 'pull timing immediately')
4. "What AFR target do you run for pump gas WOT on a 170k motor?" (Expect 11.8-12.2, not 12.5+)
5. "Do you offer separate daily + sport calibrations?" (Essential for my build)
6. "How do you handle flex fuel tuning?" (For Tune #3 specifically)
7. "Can I see your shop and dyno before booking?" (Weed out garage hack operations)
8. "What's your lead time?" (Tells me how busy and reputable they are)
9. "What's included in the price? Revisions? Follow-up datalog review?" (Clarifies scope)
10. "Can you refuse to push the car past safe limits even if I ask?" (The right answer is yes)
