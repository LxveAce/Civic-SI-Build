## Why These Mods

At ~170k miles, the stock clutch was worn and needed replacement. The Exedy OEM Stage 1 is a direct OEM-spec replacement — same friction material, same clamping force, same feel as the factory clutch.

The Acuity lightweight flywheel replaces the heavy stock dual-mass flywheel with a significantly lighter single-mass chromoly unit. This provides:
- **Faster revs:** Less rotational mass means the engine spins up and down more quickly. VTEC engagement feels sharper, rev-matching is easier.
- **Improved throttle response:** Less inertia to overcome — the engine responds more immediately to throttle input.
- **Quicker shifts:** The engine RPM drops and rises faster between shifts, making the Acuity short shifter feel even more responsive.
- **Slightly harsher idle:** The stock dual-mass flywheel smooths out combustion pulses. A single-mass lightweight flywheel transmits more vibration at idle. This is normal and mild with the Acuity unit.

## Power Limit / Reliability

The Exedy KHC10 OEM Stage 1 is the weak link for future power gains. Here's the realistic breakdown:

| Power Level | Torque (est.) | Clutch Status |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Stock (197 HP / 139 lb-ft) | 139 lb-ft | Well within limits |
| Bolt-ons + tune (210-220 WHP) | 155-165 lb-ft | Comfortable. No issues. |
| ~230 WHP | ~170 lb-ft | Upper edge of reliability. May start to see occasional slip under hard WOT in high gears on hot days. |
| ~250 WHP | ~180+ lb-ft | Slipping is likely, especially in 3rd-5th gear WOT. Time for an upgraded clutch. |

**Bottom line:** The Exedy OEM Stage 1 is reliably good to **~230 WHP / ~170 lb-ft TQ** on the K20Z3 before slip becomes a concern. With your current and planned bolt-ons (intake + headers + exhaust + tune), you'll make approximately **210-225 WHP** — well within the clutch's capability. You won't need to upgrade unless you go forced induction.

## Notes

- The lightweight flywheel makes the clutch engagement feel slightly more abrupt — less "cushion" than the stock dual-mass. This is normal and you get used to it quickly.
- Combined with the Acuity short shifter, trans-side bushings, and Hybrid Racing 70A mounts, this creates a complete drivetrain feel transformation.
- The lighter flywheel technically reduces low-RPM drivability slightly (less momentum to keep the engine spinning through lugging situations). Avoid lugging the engine in high gear at low RPM.
