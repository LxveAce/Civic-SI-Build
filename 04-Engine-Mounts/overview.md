## Why This Mod

At 170k miles, the stock rubber engine mounts are likely degraded and soft. Stiffer mounts reduce engine movement under load, which:
- Improves shift feel (less engine rock during gear changes)
- Improves throttle response (less drivetrain slop)
- Reduces wheel hop under hard launches
- Complements the Acuity short shifter and bushings for an overall tighter drivetrain feel

## Trade-Offs

70A durometer is on the **streetable end** of performance mounts:

| Durometer | Character |
|-----------|-----------|
| Stock rubber (~40-50A) | Smooth, quiet, isolates all vibration |
| **70A (this kit)** | **Noticeable vibration at idle, moderate NVH increase, significant improvement in drivetrain feel** |
| 85-90A | Harsh at idle, very noticeable cabin vibration, track-oriented |
| Solid (100A+) | Race car. Unbearable for street use. |

**Expect:** A noticeable buzz/vibration at idle and low RPM, especially felt through the steering wheel, shift knob, and pedals. At driving RPMs it disappears. Most owners of 70A mounts on the 8th gen SI find them a worthwhile trade-off for the improved driving feel.

## Notes

- No tuning impact — purely mechanical
- Install requires supporting the engine from above (engine support bar) or below (jack under oil pan with wood block) while swapping mounts
- Typical install time: 2-4 hours
