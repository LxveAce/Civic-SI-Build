# Cylinder Head — Aftermarket Drop-In Head + Valvetrain

## Status: Research Complete, Not Yet Purchased

## The Decision

I decided to go with an **aftermarket drop-in head**, not a stock-head refresh. The "go all out" call on the head side of this build — while keeping reliability-first intact and not buying absolute top-tier race parts.

## What The Research Actually Says (Important Counter-Point)

Before the recommendation: **at my target of 220-240 WHP pump / 230-250 WHP E85, a refreshed stock RBC head would hit the number**. The stock K20Z3 head flows ~290 CFM at stock valve size, which supports ~260 WHP NA all day when paired with headers, IM, and tune. The FBO package doesn't run out of head at 250 WHP.

**The drop-in head is a ceiling-expansion decision.** It buys ~20-30 WHP of future headroom, makes the next upgrade step (bigger cams, higher CR pistons, aggressive E85 tune) bolt-on rather than teardown-again, and gives me the option to move beyond 270 WHP NA later without pulling the engine a second time.

Given I'm going all-in on this build, the drop-in path is the right move. Documented here because I want future-me (or anyone reading this) to understand the trade.

## Critical Casting Compatibility Check (don't skip this)

**The K20Z3 is an RBC casting, NOT PRB.** This matters for head selection:

- **RBC** (K20Z3 Civic Si 06-11 + K24A2 US TSX 04-08): the casting I have
- **PRB** (K20A2/K20Z1 Acura RSX Type-S 02-06, JDM DC5 Integra R): different coolant routing, different IM bolt pattern

Several popular drop-in heads are **PRB-casting-based** in their base product:
- 4Piston Pro 156v2 **bare CNC** head — PRB base
- Iconic PRB K20 CNC Head — PRB-only
- Some Drag Cartel listings

What works for RBC (K20Z3):
- ✅ **4 Piston Pro 156v2 All Motor Package** (assembled) — RBC-compatible per 4P's fitment list
- ✅ 4 Piston Pro 163 — same fitment envelope
- ✅ KMOD Stage 2 K20 Head — RBC or PRB core accepted
- ✅ P2R RBC K-Series CNC Head — specifically RBC
- ✅ Portflow custom work — RBC core accepted via core exchange

**Before buying anything, I will email the vendor with "This is going on a K20Z3 RBC-casting block — is this part correct for that casting?" and get the confirmation in writing.** Mis-orders on this exact casting question are the #1 forum complaint.

## My Pick: 4 Piston Pro 156v2 All Motor Package

**$2,740 assembled, RBC-compatible, valvetrain included.**

Why:
1. **Assembled with matched components** — Ferrea valves + Supertech 82lb dual springs + Ti retainers + locks, all paired by the shop that built the head. No "mix and match" risk.
2. **CNC-ported with NEWEN 5-angle valve job** — repeatable, documented flow numbers (~360 CFM)
3. **Manganese bronze guides + oversized steel intake seats** — durable for E85 moisture, good for 200k+ more miles
4. **Industry-leading shop reputation** — 4Piston has held All-Motor records for a decade
5. **RBC-compatible** (confirmed before order — see section above)
6. **Lead time bite-sized** — assembled packages ship faster than custom CNC work

Alternatives I considered and rejected:
- **KMOD Stage 2 + DIY valvetrain** ($1,750): saves $1,000 but requires me to source Supertech separately, do my own install of springs/retainers, and trust the match. Also Stage 2 is the right head, but I'm paying 4Piston for the assembled certainty.
- **Drag Cartel Street/Strip + OG combo** ($4,999): over-spec. The 5-15 WHP extra over 4P 156v2 isn't worth the premium for 250 WHP.
- **Portflow** ($1,500–2,200): I'd save ~$500 but accept 2–6 month lead time. Not acceptable given my mod timeline.

### Head Studs — ARP 208-4701

- **Part number: ARP 208-4701** (K-series Pro Series, 8740 chrome-moly, 190,000 PSI)
- Price: ~$185–210
- **Don't order 208-4303** — that's the B-series GSR kit, wrong engine
- Torque procedure: 40 / 60 / 80 ft-lb in 3 passes, spiral from center outward, with ARP Ultra-Torque lube (included)
- **Skip the 625+ upgrade** — standard 208-4701 handles 500+ WHP on E85. 625+ ($400–500) is for 500+ WHP turbo builds, not this.

## Install Considerations

### Engine-Pull vs Head-Off In-Car

**Head-off in-car is doable on the FG2.** Transverse mount is tight but clearance exists. Remove IM + fuel rail + headers, remove timing chain cover via passenger fender well, remove valve cover + cams + head bolts, lift head straight up with two-person hoist. Hood and wiper cowl stay on.

Time estimates:
- First-time DIY in-car: 3–5 days weekend work
- Experienced indie shop: 12–16 hours = **$1,200–1,800** at $100–150/hr
- Engine pull adds 8–12 hours (~$800–1,200) — **not warranted** since bottom end stays stock

Tools beyond what I already have:
- Engine hoist rental (for the head lift, ~45 lb)
- Honda timing chain holder / cam alignment tool (Lisle 37950, $40)
- ARP torque wrench 80 ft-lb capable (already have)
- VTC actuator wrench ($50 or shop-made)

### Tune Implications — Mandatory Dyno Retune

A drop-in head changes:
- Volumetric efficiency curve (more flow at higher lift / longer duration)
- VTC engagement window (cams dictate VTEC worth point)
- Ignition timing requirements (faster burn = less advance in peak region, sometimes more in transition)
- Fuel requirements (more air = more fuel, injector duty math changes)

**Budget $500–800 for a proper dyno retune** at a Hondata-capable shop. See [`10-Hondata-FlashPro/tuning-workflow-and-maps.md`](../10-Hondata-FlashPro/tuning-workflow-and-maps.md).

**Do NOT drive on the street before retune.** Fuel maps for new cams/head on old tune = lean WOT = cracked piston. Gentle 10-mile idle/break-in loop to dyno, then retune.

## Risks I'm Accepting

1. **Bottom end has 170k miles.** A fresh head won't stress the bottom end beyond stock levels (still 250 WHP, not 400). But tell-tale bottom-end noise (rod knock, main bearing whine, piston slap) will be less masked by a quieter top end. Accept and monitor.

2. **Port-matching downstream is required.** Head → IM port floor, header → head flange. A 0.5mm step is common and costs 3–5 WHP. 20 minutes with a porting burr at install fixes it — see [`13-Headers/install-guide.md`](../13-Headers/install-guide.md).

3. **Chamber CC may shift.** If the ported head unshrouds aggressively, CC climbs from stock ~35cc to ~37-38cc, dropping static CR from 11.0:1 to ~10.5-10.7:1. Costs 2–4 WHP NA. 4Piston will document as-machined chamber CC on request.

4. **Lead time.** Email Luke at 4Piston first. If lead time pushes out the install date unacceptably, KMOD Stage 2 is the faster fallback.

5. **E85 seat compatibility.** Confirm with vendor that seats are hardened for E85 moisture. Modern Supertech / Ferrea / OEM Honda valves all are. Just verify before clicking buy.

6. **"Head done but not tuned" is the danger window.** Do not drive more than gentle 10-mile break-in between install and dyno. Closed-loop learning on wrong cam/head data corrupts trims.
