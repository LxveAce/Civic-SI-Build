# Strut Bar Install Guide — Megan Racing Race Spec (Deferred Until Post-Coilover)

## Status: DEFERRED — not blocked. Do NOT install until coilovers are in.

The Megan Racing bar (MR-SB-HC06FU-1P) is in the box on my shelf. I'm not installing it and I'm not troubleshooting fitment yet either. This is the cleanest "right part, wrong time" situation in my entire build. The part was bundled into the $960.43 clutch/flywheel/strut bar purchase from shop.redline360.com, so it's paid for — but it's NOT going on before the coilovers.

---

## Reality Check

The install itself is 30-45 minutes. Lift hood, shop towel across strut towers, drop bar on, torque 4 bolts, verify clearance, close hood. If the part fits the final suspension geometry, this is one of the easiest mods on my list.

**The work is not the install — it's the decision of WHEN.** I almost test-fit the bar against my stock strut mounts a few weeks back and only stopped because I remembered the coilovers were coming. Any data I collect on clearance TODAY is wrong data.

---

## Why Deferred

Cross-reference [17-Suspension/overview.md](../17-Suspension/overview.md) — my coilover pick is the BC Racing BR (A-18-BR), which includes **pillow-ball camber plates** that replace the stock upper strut mount entirely. Stock mount = thick rubber isolator with steel top cap, ~1" tall, 3-nut pattern. BR camber plate = lower-profile spherical bearing housing, different top-deck height, adjustable bolt spacing via the camber slot.

A strut bar interacts with exactly that surface: the mounting studs, the top-cap height (which sets how tall the bar's end brackets sit), and clearance to adjacent engine-bay components. Every one of those variables changes when the BR goes in. Three outcomes are on the table, all unknowable until the coilovers are installed and aligned:

- Bar fits perfectly post-coilover.
- Bar needs a spacer under one or both end brackets for the new top-deck height.
- Bar doesn't fit at all — shop for a different bar that's known to clear pillow-ball camber plates.

Reliability-first rule: installing chassis bracing that doesn't account for final suspension geometry leads to a rattling bar, bolts loosening over time from a wrong load path, or worst case, pre-loading the towers in a direction they weren't designed to carry. The 8th gen strut tower sheet metal isn't heavy gauge. I'm not finding out the hard way that a forced-fit bar cracked a weld.

## Pre-Install Fitment Check (DO FIRST, BEFORE REMOVING ANY BOLTS)

The single most important step on the page. I am NOT removing any existing fasteners until the bar test-fits without being forced.

1. Hood up. Shop towel across both strut towers.
2. Lift the bar out of the box. Note orientation — MR bars have an asymmetric bend to clear the brake booster on LHD cars. Identify driver side.
3. Rest the bar across both towers WITHOUT threading any fasteners. Let it sit on its own.
4. Check clearance visually and with a ruler to every adjacent component:
   - **Brake master cylinder / booster** (driver side — biggest clearance offender on 8th gen coupes)
   - **Fuel rail cover / intake manifold top**
   - **Cowl panel** (plastic trim below windshield)
   - **Intake tube** — my [K&N Typhoon](../01-Cold-Air-Intake/) runs hot-side; confirm no contact
   - **ABS module** (passenger side corner)
   - Any wiring harnesses or vacuum lines across the bay
5. Close hood slowly. Stop at any resistance. Check for hood-pad contact.
6. Photograph any interference points.

**Decision point:**

- No interference, bar sits flat: proceed.
- Minor interference (hood pad touching, one wire rubbing): proceed, plan to trim pad foam or reroute wire.
- Severe interference (bar won't sit flat, booster in the way, bottoms out on cowl): STOP. See alternatives at bottom.

## Step-by-Step (post-coilover, assuming fitment check passed)

### Phase A: Prep (5-10 min)

1. Engine cold, hood up and propped.
2. Remove intake tube or airbox if needed for passenger-side strut-top access. My K&N Typhoon's MAF section may need to come out for socket clearance — see [01-Cold-Air-Intake/](../01-Cold-Air-Intake/) for the removal procedure. Reinstall after.
3. Shop towel across both towers — aluminum bar will scratch paint instantly on contact.
4. Dry-fit one more time with the towel in place.

### Phase B: Strut-Top Fastener Prep (5-10 min)

Factory 8th gen SI uses 3 nuts per strut tower. The BR camber plate may use the same pattern or a 2-bolt camber-slot design — I'll know once the coilovers are in.

5. **DO NOT remove all strut-top nuts on both sides simultaneously.** The suspension loads against those fasteners; removing them all lets the strut push down into the knuckle. One side at a time, one nut at a time.
6. Loosen — do NOT fully remove — the nut(s) that the MR end bracket will bolt to. Most MR designs slip the end bracket over an existing strut-top stud and reuse the OEM nut.
7. If the MR design uses its own pass-through hardware instead of the strut-top studs, leave the strut-top nuts alone. Megan's instruction sheet is the authority — verify before touching anything.

### Phase C: Bar Placement (5 min)

8. Lower the bar onto both towers in the orientation confirmed during fitment check.
9. Align pass-through holes with the prepared fasteners.
10. Thread fasteners on BOTH sides loosely, hand-tight, equal thread engagement.
11. Verify the bar sits flat. Any gap between end bracket and tower means the geometry is fighting me — tightening fasteners would pull the chassis into the bar's shape. That's the exact outcome I'm trying to avoid. STOP if I see a gap.

### Phase D: Torque Sequence (5-10 min)

12. Center the bar — equal distance to any engine-bay reference point from each end. A shifted bar loads the towers asymmetrically.
13. **Target torque: 30-35 ft-lb.** Verify against Megan's printed sheet — their spec wins if it differs. Factory strut-top nut spec is 29 ft-lb ([docs/torque-specs.md](../docs/torque-specs.md)), so this range is consistent.
14. Three passes alternating sides between bolts:
    - Pass 1: ~15 ft-lb
    - Pass 2: ~25 ft-lb
    - Pass 3: final 30-35 ft-lb
15. Grab the bar at center, wiggle up-down. Should feel completely rigid. Any flex = loose fastener; find it.

### Phase E: Clearance Verification (5 min)

16. Hood closes and latches fully, no resistance.
17. Open hood, confirm no contact between bar and brake lines, harnesses, vacuum lines, coolant hoses, or hood pad.
18. Key on (engine off). Steering lock-to-lock both ways — no wheel-well liner interference, no rubbing. If the bar somehow affects steering, STOP.
19. Reinstall any intake components that came off for access.

## First Drive Checklist

Short low-speed drive around the block. Listening and feeling for:

- No squeaks or rattles from the strut-tower area over bumps
- No knocking when loading the front suspension (driveway entry, speed bumps)
- Steering feels the same or slightly crisper on turn-in — chassis bracing does not change steering effort. If it feels heavier, something is pre-loaded wrong.
- Brake pedal feel unchanged. If the bar is contacting the booster, pedal feel changes — that means I missed a clearance check.

If all four pass: drive normally and re-check fastener torque after 100 miles.

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Forcing the bar into place when it doesn't naturally fit.** Prying, bending, or cranking fasteners to bridge a gap pre-loads the towers against each other. Cracked welds at the tower-to-apron junction are a known outcome.
- **Removing all strut-top nuts on both sides at once.** Unloads the suspension in an uncontrolled way.
- **Skipping the fitment check before coilovers are installed.** I almost did this. The data would have been garbage.
- **Torquing in wrong sequence.** Single-pass of one side then the other pulls the bar sideways as each side seats. Always alternate.
- **Not re-checking torque after 100 miles.** Chassis flex seats everything; fasteners relax slightly.

## If Fitment Fails Post-Coilover

No grinding or bending the MR bar to force it. If it truly doesn't fit with the BR camber plates, the Megan goes on the shelf as a spare or gets resold and I shop for a known-good alternative:

- **Cusco Type OS Ti 2-point front strut bar** — designed for JDM cars running aggressive camber, known to clear most pillow-ball plates. Higher cost, proven fitment.
- **Ultra Racing Hard URTW2-003** — community reports of clearance on lowered FG2 coupes. Mid-priced.
- **Custom triangulated bar from a specialist fab shop** — nuclear option. Match the camber-plate bolt pattern exactly. $300-500, overkill for street, guaranteed to fit.

Decision happens after the coilovers are in, after alignment, and after one more try with the MR bar against the final geometry.
